FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Is weight lifting safe for beginners?

Absolutely! When taught properly with appropriate weights, weight lifting is safe for beginners. Our coaches focus on teaching proper form before adding significant weight. We start all beginners with a movement assessment and gradually progress the difficulty as you build strength and confidence.

How often should I lift weights?

For beginners, we recommend 3 full-body workouts per week with at least one rest day between sessions. Intermediate lifters typically train 4-5 days per week with more specialization. Advanced athletes may train 5-6 days per week with carefully planned recovery periods.

Will weight lifting make me bulky?

Not unless you specifically train and eat to gain significant muscle mass. Weight lifting will make you stronger and more toned, but substantial muscle growth requires dedicated effort with a calorie surplus. Women especially don't need to worry about getting "too big" as they lack the testosterone levels required for extreme muscle growth.

What should I bring to my first session?

Wear comfortable workout clothes and flat-soled shoes (like Converse or weightlifting shoes). Bring a water bottle and towel. We provide all necessary equipment. Arrive 10-15 minutes early to complete paperwork and meet your coach.

How long until I see results?

Most beginners notice strength improvements within 2-4 weeks. Visible muscle changes typically take 6-8 weeks of consistent training. The key factors are consistency with your workouts, proper nutrition, and adequate recovery.
