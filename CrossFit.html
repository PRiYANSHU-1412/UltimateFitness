WHAT IS CROSSFIT?

CrossFit is a high-intensity fitness program incorporating elements from several sports and types of exercise. Our program delivers a fitness that is, by design, broad, general, and inclusive.

At Ultimate Fitness, we focus on constantly varied functional movements performed at high intensity. All CrossFit workouts are based on functional movements, and these movements reflect the best aspects of gymnastics, weightlifting, running, rowing, and more.

Our CrossFit program is designed for universal scalability, making it the perfect application for any committed individual regardless of experience. We scale load and intensity; we don't change the program.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Is CrossFit right for beginners?

Absolutely! CrossFit is designed to be scalable for all fitness levels. Our coaches will modify workouts to match your current abilities while helping you progress safely. Every movement can be adjusted in weight, range of motion, or intensity to suit your needs.

How often should I do CrossFit?

For beginners, we recommend 3-4 times per week to allow for proper recovery. More experienced athletes can train 5-6 times per week. Rest days are crucial for progress and injury prevention.

What should I bring to my first class?

Wear comfortable workout clothes and athletic shoes. Bring a water bottle and towel. All equipment is provided. We recommend arriving 10-15 minutes early for your first session to complete paperwork and meet your coach.

How is CrossFit different from regular gym training?

CrossFit emphasizes functional movements performed at high intensity with constant variation. Unlike traditional gym workouts, CrossFit combines strength training, cardio, and gymnastics in constantly varied workouts with a strong community aspect and coach-led sessions.

Will CrossFit make me bulky?

Not unless you specifically train and eat to become bulky. CrossFit develops lean, functional muscle that enhances athletic performance rather than just size. Women especially don't need to worry about getting "too big" as they lack the testosterone levels required for significant muscle hypertrophy.
